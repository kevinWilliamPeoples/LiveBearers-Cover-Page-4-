Livebearers

What are 'Livebearers'?

Livebearers are the fish that give birth to live, free-swimming young(fry) instead of laying eggs.
Livebearers generally reach a maximum size around 2 to 3 inches, though the exact details vary from one species to another, and they are generally peaceful in terms of their temperament. They are an excellent community fish and can tolerate a range of temperatures between 64°F and 82°F as well as a pH between 5.5 and 8.0.

Following are the names some of the most popular livebearing aquarium fish:

1) Molly

2) Guppy

3) Platy

4) Swordtail

5) Endler's livebearer

6) Mosquito fish

NOTE: All the fish mentioned above are freshwater.

What Are the Pros and Cons of Livebearers?

[A] Pros of livebearers

  1. Most species are very peaceful by nature, great for community tanks
  2. These fish breed readily and continuously with little effort on your part
  3. Many species are adaptable to a range of temperatures and water chemistry
  4. These fish are easy to care for and maintain in the home aquarium
  5. Most species grow no larger than 2 to 3 inches in length

[B] of livebearers

  1. Continuous breeding can lead to overcrowding if you aren’t careful
  2. These fish require a varied diet of fresh, frozen, and commercial foods to breed well
  3. Most livebearers do best in schools so you may need a large tank to accommodate them
  4. These fish will eat their own young unless you separate them from the parents
